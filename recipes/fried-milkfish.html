Fried Milkfish

This recipe offers a straightforward method for preparing flavorful fried milkfish (bangus) with a simple marinade. The milkfish is soaked in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, black pepper, and minced garlic, allowing it to absorb these savory and slightly tangy notes. After marinating, the fish is fried in hot oil until both sides are cooked through and likely crispy, resulting in a delicious and easy-to-prepare Filipino dish. The brief ingredient list and uncomplicated cooking steps make this a convenient way to enjoy the distinct taste of milkfish.

Ingredients

  • Milkfish
  • Soy sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Black pepper
  • Minced garlic
  • Oil

Cooking Steps

  1. Marinate the milkfish with a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, black pepper, and minced garlic for an hour or more.
  2. Heat the oil with medium-high heat to the point where it is hot enough to sizzle when we add the milkfish.
  3. Carefully place the milkfish to the hot oil skin side first and let it fry for 6-8 minutes.
  4. Flip the milkfish onto the meat side and let it fry for another 6-8 minutes.
  5. Serve.
