Shrimp Sinigang Recipe

This recipe offers a simplified approach to the beloved Filipino sour soup, Sinigang na Hipon. It features the essential tangy flavor derived from a tamarind soup base mix, combined with the savory notes of fish sauce. The dish incorporates readily available vegetables such as tomatoes (kamatis), onions (sibuyas), ginger (luya), string beans (sitaw), water spinach (kangkong), and green pepper (siling pansigang), all simmered in water until tender. Shrimp are added towards the end, ensuring they remain succulent. This straightforward method delivers the comforting and distinctly sour taste of Sinigang with minimal fuss, making it a quick and easy way to enjoy this classic Filipino favorite.

Ingredients

  • Shrimps
  • Tomatoes(Kamatis)
  • Onions(Sibuyas)
  • Ginger(Luya)
  • String beans(Sitaw)
  • Water spinach(Kangkong)
  • Green pepper(Siling pangsigang)
  • Fish sauce(Patis)
  • Tamarind mix
  • Water

Cooking Steps

  1. Boil the water in a pot.
  2. Slice the tomotoes, onions, ginger into smaller pieces.
  3. (Optional) Peel the skin of the shirmp.
  4. Once the water is boiling, add the sliced vegetables and let it simmer until it softens.
  5. Season the broth with tamarind mix and fish sauce.
  6. Add the remaining vegetables and the shrimp and let it cook for 3-5 minutes.
  7. Serve.
